DUMBBELLS: ORIGIN, TYPES AND HISTORY

The Dumbbell, also known as hand weights and free weights, is undeniably one of the most common equipment utilized for weight training. It is usually used in pairs but can also be used individually. It consists of a narrow single bar in the middle with equal weights on each end. Some dumbbells are very light in weight while others are very heavy. With this, dumbbells vary from only a few pounds to as heavy as 50 pounds or more. In addition to that, they can be fixed in weight or variable. Weight discs can be added or removed from the bar by quantity or by size. Most people think that this type of equipment has only been in the modern ages. However, this versatile piece of free weight tool has been used for a variety of exercises since 2,000 years ago.

As stated above, the dumbbell has been used for weight training in order to tone and increase the muscle size. They are frequently used by bodybuilders, powerlifters, athletes, and other exercise enthusiasts within their workout routines. Each exercise designed using the dumbbell targets a specific group of muscles. With comprehensive and regular exercise, muscles of the arms, shoulders, back, chest, legs, and core can be developed in order to be more toned and strong.

ORIGIN

In Early 18th century: originally denoting an apparatus similar to that used to ring a church bell (but without the bell, so noiseless or ‘dumb’); dumb-bell.
(sense 2): (dating from the 1920s) is an extended use by association with dumb ‘stupid’.
